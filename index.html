Google Analytics

How many people actually visit our website, and how many people actually read our emails?

Prior to 2016, Ngee Ann Polytechnic’s website was being tracked by a server-side analytics tool that was not user-friendly and did not meet our needs.

As we did not have in-house capabilities, I was offered the role to explore and implement web analytics for our digital platforms.

After obtaining my Google Analytics Individual Qualification within 2 days, I implemented Google Analytics via Google Tag Manager, and integrated all our website and microsites into a single account so that we can track visitors’ journey across all our web properties.

We also started using Custom Campaigns to track the open rates of our email campaigns. We were not tracking our email campaigns previously and had no data at all.

During our website revamp, we also used the page visit data to determine which web pages to remove.

e-Newsletter

Before I joined the organisation, the weekly e-newsletter was done painstakingly in Word, then pasted into an email. It was not mobile-responsive, did not have a web version, and took up to a full day’s work.

The teammates had to submit the information in an Excel file hosted on a shared server; when a user is logged in, another user cannot access the file until the first user logs out.

I automated the workflow and moved it onto the web – teammates now submit information using a web-based form which can be accessed by anyone, anywhere. The information is then saved in CSV format and imported into the website, saving time and effort.

The new e-newsletter is now mobile-responsive, can be accessed anytime, anywhere without checking the email inbox, shareable, and help to drive traffic to the website.
